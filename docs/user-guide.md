## 1. Welcome to CoverVerifi

CoverVerifi is a simple, modern tool built for construction consultants and general contractors in Idaho who need to stay on top of subcontractor insurance compliance.

If you manage subcontractors, you know the headache: tracking certificates of insurance, making sure workers' comp and general liability policies are current, chasing down insurance agents for verification, and keeping everything organized before draw payments go out.

CoverVerifi takes care of all of that in one place. With CoverVerifi, you can:

- **Track insurance certificates** for every subcontractor you work with.
- **See at a glance** which subs are compliant, which policies are about to expire, and which are missing coverage entirely.
- **Send verification requests** directly to insurance agents by email, so you can confirm coverage without phone calls or faxes.
- **Stay compliant** with Idaho workers' compensation and liability requirements, including Idaho Code Section 72-212(4) sole proprietor exemptions.
- **Manage multiple general contractors** if you are a compliance consultant overseeing several GC clients.

Whether you are an admin managing the entire system or a general contractor keeping tabs on your own subcontractors, CoverVerifi gives you a clear, up-to-date picture of your compliance status.

## 3. Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first thing you see after logging in. It gives you a quick summary of where things stand across all your subcontractors and their insurance coverage.

### Stats Cards

At the top of the Dashboard, you will see a row of cards showing key numbers:

- **Total Contractors** -- How many general contractors are in the system.
- **Total Subcontractors** -- How many subcontractors are being tracked.
- **Compliant** -- How many subcontractors have all their insurance in order.
- **Expiring** -- How many subcontractors have policies that will expire within the next 30 days.
- **Expired** -- How many subcontractors have policies that are past due.
- **Missing** -- How many subcontractors are missing required coverage entirely.

### Compliance Rate Bar

Below the stats cards, you will see a **compliance rate bar**. This shows the overall percentage of subcontractors who are fully compliant. The higher the number, the better shape you are in.

### Policies Needing Attention

This section lists policies that require action -- ones that are expiring soon, already expired, or missing. Click on any item in this list to jump directly to the policy details so you can take action.

### Recent Subcontractors

This section shows the most recently added or updated subcontractors. Click on any name to view their full profile, or click the link to view all subcontractors.

## 4. Managing General Contractors (Admin Only)

> **Note:** This section is only available to Admin users. If you are logged in as a General Contractor, you will not see the contractor management area.

The **General Contractors** page lets you manage all of the GC companies in the system.

### Viewing Contractors

You will see a table listing all general contractors. You can:

- **Search** by typing a company name or contact name into the search bar.
- **Sort** by clicking on any column header (company name, contact, status, etc.).
- **Browse** through pages if you have many contractors.

### Adding a New Contractor

1. Click the **"Add Contractor"** button at the top of the page.
2. Fill in the required information:
   - Company name
   - Primary contact name
   - Email address
   - Phone number
   - Any additional details
3. Click **"Save"** to create the contractor.

### Viewing Contractor Details

Click on any row in the table to open that contractor's detail page. Here you can see their full contact information and a list of all subcontractors assigned to them.

### Activating or Deactivating a Contractor

From the contractor detail page, you can toggle a contractor between **Active** and **Inactive** status. Deactivating a contractor does not delete their data -- it simply marks them as inactive so they no longer appear in active lists.

## 5. Managing Subcontractors

The **Subcontractors** page is where you manage the companies and individuals doing work for your general contractors.

### Who Sees What

- **Admins** see all subcontractors across every general contractor.
- **General Contractors** see only the subcontractors assigned to them.

### Sole Proprietor Warning Banner

At the top of the subcontractors page, you may see a blue information banner about sole proprietors. This is an important note about **Idaho Code Section 72-212(4)**, which provides a workers' compensation exemption for sole proprietors who have no employees. If a subcontractor is a sole proprietor working alone, they may be exempt from carrying workers' comp insurance under Idaho law. The banner is there to help you understand this distinction when reviewing compliance status.

### Adding a New Subcontractor

1. Click the **"Add Subcontractor"** button.
2. Fill in the details:
   - Company name
   - Primary contact name
   - Email address
   - Phone number
   - Trade or specialty (e.g., Electrical, Plumbing, Framing)
   - Whether they are a sole proprietor
   - Any other relevant information
3. Click **"Save"** to add them to the system.

### Viewing Subcontractor Details

Click on any row in the subcontractors table to open their full profile. On the detail page, you will find:

- **Contact information** -- name, email, phone, trade.
- **Insurance policies on file** -- a list of all certificates of insurance associated with this subcontractor, along with their compliance status.
- **Assigned General Contractors** -- which GCs this subcontractor is linked to.
- **Compliance status** -- an at-a-glance indicator showing whether all required coverage is current.

## 6. Viewing Insurance Policies

The **Insurance Policies** page gives you a complete view of every certificate of insurance in the system.

### Filtering Policies

Use the filter buttons at the top to narrow down what you see:

- **All** -- Every policy in the system.
- **Compliant** -- Only policies that are current and meet requirements.
- **Expiring** -- Policies that will expire within the next 30 days.
- **Expired** -- Policies that are past their expiration date.

### Idaho Compliance Defaults

You will see a blue information box on this page showing the default compliance requirements for Idaho. These are the minimum coverage levels and policy types that CoverVerifi checks against, based on Idaho workers' compensation and liability standards.

### Viewing Policy Details

Click on any policy to see its full details:

- **Carrier** -- The insurance company providing the coverage.
- **Policy number** -- The unique identifier for this policy.
- **Coverage type and amounts** -- What is covered and for how much (e.g., General Liability, Workers' Compensation).
- **Effective dates** -- When the policy starts and when it expires.
- **Agent information** -- The insurance agent or agency handling this policy.
- **Verification history** -- A log of all verification requests that have been sent for this policy and their outcomes.

### Sending a Verification Request

On the policy detail page, you will see a **"Send Verification"** button. Clicking this will send an email to the insurance agent on file, asking them to confirm that the coverage is active and current. This is the fastest way to verify a certificate without picking up the phone.

## 8. Verification Requests

The **Verification Requests** page tracks every request that has been sent to an insurance agent asking them to confirm coverage.

### Filtering Requests

Use the filter buttons to view:

- **All** -- Every verification request.
- **Pending** -- Requests that have been sent but not yet responded to.
- **Confirmed** -- Requests where the agent has confirmed the coverage is active.
- **Uploaded** -- Requests where the agent has uploaded an updated certificate.

### Stats Bar

At the top of the page, you will see a summary showing:

- **Total** requests sent
- **Pending** requests awaiting response
- **Confirmed** requests completed
- **Uploaded** certificates received

### Resending a Request

If a verification request is still **Pending** and you have not heard back from the agent, click the **"Resend"** button next to that request. This will send a follow-up email to the agent reminding them to respond.

## 10. Common Workflows

Here are step-by-step instructions for the things you will do most often in CoverVerifi.

### How to Add a New Subcontractor

1. Click **"Subcontractors"** in the navigation menu on the left side of the screen.
2. Click the **"Add Subcontractor"** button in the upper right corner.
3. Fill in the company name, contact name, email, phone, and trade.
4. If the sub is a sole proprietor with no employees, check the sole proprietor box.
5. Select which general contractor(s) this sub works for.
6. Click **"Save"** to add the subcontractor.
7. You will be taken to the new subcontractor's detail page, where you can add their insurance information.

### How to Check If a Subcontractor Is Compliant

1. Click **"Subcontractors"** in the navigation menu.
2. Find the subcontractor you want to check. You can use the search bar or scroll through the list.
3. Look at the **compliance status indicator** next to their name:
   - **Green** means they are fully compliant.
   - **Yellow** means a policy is expiring within 30 days.
   - **Red** means a policy has expired.
   - **Gray** means coverage is missing entirely.
4. Click on the subcontractor's row to open their detail page for more specific information about which policies are current and which need attention.

### How to Send a Verification to an Agent

1. Navigate to the subcontractor whose coverage you want to verify.
2. On their detail page, find the insurance policy you want to verify.
3. Click on the policy to open its detail view.
4. Click the **"Send Verification"** button.
5. The system will send an email to the insurance agent on file, asking them to confirm the coverage is active.
6. You can track the status of your request on the **Verification Requests** page.

### How to Review Expiring Policies

1. Go to the **Dashboard** and look at the **"Expiring"** stat card to see how many policies are expiring soon.
2. Click on the **"Policies Needing Attention"** section, or navigate to the **Insurance Policies** page.
3. Click the **"Expiring"** filter to show only policies expiring within 30 days.
4. For each expiring policy, you can:
   - Click to view the full details.
   - Click **"Send Verification"** to ask the agent to confirm renewed coverage.
   - Make a note to follow up before the expiration date.

## 11. Understanding Compliance Status Colors

Throughout CoverVerifi, you will see color-coded indicators that tell you the compliance status of a subcontractor or policy at a glance. Here is what each color means:

| Color | Status | What It Means |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Green** | Compliant | The policy is current and meets all requirements. No action needed. |
| **Yellow** | Expiring Soon | The policy will expire within the next 30 days. You should follow up to ensure it gets renewed. |
| **Red** | Expired | The policy expiration date has passed. This subcontractor is out of compliance and should not be working until coverage is restored. |
| **Gray** | Not on File | There is no policy on file for this subcontractor. Coverage information needs to be added or obtained. |

When you see yellow, red, or gray indicators, that is your signal to take action -- send a verification request, contact the subcontractor, or reach out to their insurance agent.

## 12. FAQ

### What is CoverVerifi?

CoverVerifi is a web-based tool that helps construction consultants and general contractors track subcontractor insurance certificates, verify coverage with agents, and stay compliant with Idaho workers' compensation and liability requirements. It replaces spreadsheets, filing cabinets, and manual follow-up with a simple, organized system.

### Who can see my data?

It depends on your role:

- **Admin users** (like a compliance consultant) can see all general contractors, all subcontractors, all policies, agents, email logs, and verification requests across the entire system.
- **General Contractor users** can see only their own subcontractors and the policies associated with those subcontractors. They cannot see other GCs' data.

Your data is kept separate and secure based on your login.

### What is a sole proprietor exemption?

Under **Idaho Code Section 72-212(4)**, a sole proprietor who has no employees is not required to carry workers' compensation insurance. This means that if one of your subcontractors works alone and has no employees, they may be legally exempt from the workers' comp requirement in Idaho. CoverVerifi flags sole proprietors so you can account for this exemption when reviewing compliance. However, you should always consult with a legal professional if you are unsure about a specific situation.

### What does "Additional Insured" mean?

When a subcontractor's insurance policy lists you (the general contractor or project owner) as an "Additional Insured," it means their policy extends coverage to you for claims arising from the subcontractor's work. This is important because it gives you a layer of protection if something goes wrong on a job. Many GCs require their subs to add them as an Additional Insured before work begins.

### How often should I verify coverage?

Best practice is to verify subcontractor insurance coverage **before every draw payment**. A draw payment is when you release funds for completed work. By verifying before each draw, you make sure you are never paying a subcontractor who has let their coverage lapse. You should also verify whenever you onboard a new subcontractor and when you receive a notification that a policy is about to expire.

## 13. Troubleshooting

### The page will not load or looks broken

- **Refresh the page** by pressing the reload button in your browser or pressing Ctrl+R (Cmd+R on Mac).
- **Clear your browser cache.** Go to your browser settings, find the option to clear browsing data, and clear cached files and images. Then try again.
- **Try a different browser.** If the issue persists, open the app in a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge).

### I cannot log in

- **Double-check your email and password.** Make sure there are no extra spaces and that Caps Lock is not on.
- **Try the demo accounts** listed in the Getting Started section above to confirm the app is working.
- **Contact your administrator** if you have forgotten your password or your account may have been deactivated.

### The data looks wrong or out of date

- **Refresh the page** to make sure you are seeing the latest information.
- **Contact your administrator.** They can review the data and make corrections if something was entered incorrectly.

### I cannot find a subcontractor I am looking for

- **Use the search bar** on the Subcontractors page to search by name.
- If the subcontractor is not in the system, they may need to be **added by an Admin** user.
- If you are a General Contractor user, the subcontractor may exist in the system but may not yet be **linked to your GC account**. Ask your administrator to assign the subcontractor to you.
